Executive Summary

Forty-two students completed the post-course survey at the end of the May 18–22 cohort. Course content and instruction land solidly positive; the application itself is the clear drag on the experience, and the free-text responses point to a single fixable root cause behind most of it.

Course Content 3.19 / 5 objectives, pacing, hands-on value
Instruction & Support 3.60 / 5 highest-rated dimension
Logistics 3.32 / 5 schedule, comms, classroom setup
Application Experience 2.46 / 5 lowest-rated — reliability drove this
Bottom line: 50% of students would recommend PACT to another analyst, 24% would not. The gap is not the curriculum — 74% agreed they can apply what they learned to real-world work, the strongest score in the survey. The gap is the delivery platform: 76% disagreed the application performed reliably during exercises, and a large share of the write-in feedback describes lost work, un-saved answers, and repeat submissions. Fix the reliability layer and the recommend-rate has real room to move.

Application Experience

This is where the survey is unambiguous. Every application question scored below the survey's overall average, and the write-in responses converge on one specific, fixable failure mode.

2.64/5
I liked the application interface and usability
2.74/5
  • Root cause: silent save failures The dominant, specific complaint is that submitted answers did not reliably save — students describe having to resubmit repeatedly, losing group work to refreshes, and switching to Word/Notepad as a workaround because "the application couldn't be trusted to save or submit our work."
  • This survey reproduced the bug live Of the 74 raw survey submissions received, 32 were exact-duplicate re-submissions of the same answers seconds to minutes apart — the true respondent count is 42. One respondent explicitly wrote: "Even for this survey it refreshed multiple times and did not save my submission." The bug the survey is measuring is the same one that generated the duplicate data underneath it.
  • Content discovery Several students had trouble locating release documents and evidence items scattered across scenario, modules, and workshop tabs — a navigation/IA gap distinct from the save-reliability issue.
"When completing assessments, challenges, or modules, the work completed by a user doesn't save. It often times takes several times to submit something. Therefore, when working as a group, we were often saving work to Microsoft Word or Notepad, because the application couldn't be trusted to save or submit our work."— Q27, app issue that affected them most
06

Recommendations

PriorityActionWhy
P1 Fix answer-submission reliability with a confirmed-save indicator and idempotent submit handling. Lowest-rated item in the survey (2.07/5, 76% disagree); directly named in the largest share of write-in comments; demonstrably duplicated 32 of this survey's own 74 submissions.
P1 Publish a full day-by-day agenda before the course starts, not during it. The single most repeated logistics and content complaint across four different open-text questions.
P2 Human-review all auto-generated quiz/module questions and answer-key ordering. Multiple independent respondents identified the same tell (dropdowns pre-ordered, MC answers clustering on "A") without prompting each other.
P2 Trim the capstone scenario's document volume, or extend the time allotted to work through it. Directly suppressed the hands-on-exercise (2.81/5) and pacing (2.81/5) scores — the two weakest Course Content items.
P3 Stand up a maintained post-course resource hub (tools, terminology, case templates). The most-requested single item across three separate open-text questions.
P3 Brief breakout-room mentor instructors on the scenario ahead of the cohort. Named gap between lead-instructor and mentor-instructor scenario familiarity.

Data & Methodology Note

How this report was built Source: MongoDB PACTv4.pactSurveySubmissions, filtered to cohortId: "pact-may-2026-2". 74 raw submission records were retrieved, all timestamped between 17:34 and 18:15 on 22 May 2026. Comparing full-response signatures found 32 exact duplicates — same answers to all 28 questions, submitted seconds to minutes apart — consistent with the save/submit reliability bug students independently reported in Section 05. All statistics in this report use the deduplicated set of 42 unique respondents (first submission kept per unique response signature). The survey is anonymous — there is no way to verify unique respondents beyond content matching, and 42 modestly exceeds the ~31–33 known course roster, plausibly reflecting UK partner students and instructional staff who also completed the survey. Three literal "test" placeholder answers were excluded from the free-text synthesis.
